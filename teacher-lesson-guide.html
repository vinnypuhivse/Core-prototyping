Lesson 5: Community And Social Justice (Ch. 13)

LESSON OVERVIEW
‚è± 60 minutes
üìö Grade 6
üìñ Reading & Writing

üìù Lesson Summary

In this lesson, students will explore themes of community and social justice through Chapter 13 of the anchor text. They will analyze how the author develops these themes and connect them to real-world issues. Students will engage in close reading, discussion, and written response activities to deepen their understanding.

Word Count: ~850 words

üéØ Objectives

üìñ
Content Objective
Students will analyze how the author develops themes of community and social justice in Chapter 13.
üí¨
Language Objective
Students will use evidence from the text to support their analysis in written and oral responses.
üß†
Social-Emotional Objective
Students will reflect on their own role in their community and consider how they can promote justice.

‚ùì Guiding Questions

What is the relationship between individual actions and community change?
How does the author use specific events in Chapter 13 to develop the theme of social justice?
What connections can we make between the text and current social justice movements?

üí° Essential Questions

What does it mean to be part of a community?
How do individuals create change in their communities?

üîó Connection to Anchor Text

This lesson focuses on Chapter 13, where the protagonist witnesses injustice in their community and must decide how to respond. This chapter serves as a turning point in the novel, connecting to the broader themes of identity, belonging, and systems of power that students have been exploring throughout the unit.

‚úÖ Lesson Preparation

Before the lesson:

  • Review Chapter 13 and identify key passages for close reading
  • Prepare anchor charts with guiding questions
  • Set up discussion groups (3-4 students per group)
  • Queue up any multimedia resources
  • Print or prepare digital copies of all handouts

üìã Lesson Steps

1
Warm-Up: Community Reflection (10 min)

Purpose: Activate prior knowledge and connect to students' own experiences with community.

Activity:

  • Display the prompt: "What does community mean to you? Share an example of when you felt part of a community."
  • Give students 3 minutes to write individually
  • Have students pair-share for 2 minutes
  • Call on 3-4 volunteers to share with the whole class
  • Connect student responses to today's focus on community in Chapter 13
2
Close Reading of Chapter 13 (20 min)

Purpose: Engage students in deep analysis of how the author develops themes.

Activity:

  • Introduce the close reading focus: theme development and evidence
  • Model annotation of the first key passage
  • Students read Chapter 13 independently, annotating for theme and character response to injustice
  • Circulate to support students and monitor comprehension
  • Pause at key moments to think-pair-share observations
3
Small Group Discussion (15 min)

Purpose: Deepen understanding through collaborative analysis.

Activity:

  • Students move into pre-assigned discussion groups
  • Display discussion protocol and guiding questions
  • Groups discuss for 10 minutes using the protocol
  • Whole class debrief: each group shares one key insight (5 min)
4
Written Response (12 min)

Purpose: Assess student understanding and ability to write with evidence.

Prompt: "How does the author develop the theme of social justice in Chapter 13? Use at least two pieces of evidence from the text to support your analysis."

5
Closing & Exit Ticket (3 min)

Exit Ticket: "What is one action you could take to promote justice in your own community?"

‚úì Assessment

Formative Assessment:

  • Monitor annotations during close reading
  • Listen to small group discussions
  • Review written responses for evidence-based analysis
  • Collect and review exit tickets
